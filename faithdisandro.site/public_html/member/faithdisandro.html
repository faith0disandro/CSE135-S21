Research Experience

Binary Star

During my second year at San Diego Mesa College I was lucky enough to be asked by Astronomy professor to take a part of the AAVSO (American Association of Variable Star Observers) research project of observing binary stars. As a part of the project each team had to order images from one of the space telescopes and collect data from the photos and analyze the data to find trends in the angles of the two stars to determine whether a star system is binary.

Exoplanet

During my first year at UCSD I was asked to be a part of the research of exoplanets that orbit stars outside of our solar system. The research involved studying photos of one of the stars from one of the space telescopes and observing whether there was a change in light from the star compared to data taken in the past.This change in light can determine wether or not a planet is orbiting the star.

Exoplanets, also known as extrasolar planets, are planets that are outside of our solar system.
